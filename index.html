Liane Galanti

I am a Research Scientist at IBM Research. I received my M.Sc. in Computer Science from Tel Aviv University, where I worked with Professor Lior Wolf. My research interests include universal representations, inductive biases, emergent capabilities of large models, adaptive learning, compressing neural networks, scaling laws, and data debiasing.

Selected Publications
Norm-based Generalization Bounds for Sparse Neural Networks
Tomer Galanti, Mengjia Xu, Liane Galanti, Tomaso Poggio
NeurIPS 2023
paper code
We derive tight generalization bounds for sparse neural networks and show how sparsity influences generalization. Our empirical results outperform prior bounds in the literature.
Comparative Generalization Bounds for Deep Neural Networks
Tomer Galanti, Liane Galanti, Ido Ben-Shaul
TMLR 2023
paper code
We introduce "effective depth" to capture how neural collapse propagates backward in networks. We show that effective depth is unrelated to architectural depth and adapts to data complexity.
Intelligence Analysis of Language Models
Liane Galanti, Ethan Baron
Preprint 2024
paper
We evaluate the ability of LLMs to perform abstract reasoning beyond language, using the ARC benchmark and diverse prompting strategies.
From Grounding to Planning: Benchmarking Bottlenecks in Web Agents with Mind2Web
Segev Shlomov, Ben Wiesel, Aviad Sela, Ido Levy, Liane Galanti, Roy Abitbol
Preprint 2024
paper
Using the Mind2Web benchmark, we isolate whether grounding or planning better predicts agent performance on natural language task execution.
